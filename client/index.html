How to Use BuildCalc
1
Blueprint Takeoff
Measure directly on uploaded PDFs and drawings

The core of BuildCalc. Upload a PDF or image of a drawing and measure linear footage, square footage, and counts directly on screen. Measurements flow straight into the Project Estimator.

  1. Click Open (or drag & drop a file onto the canvas) to load a PDF or image drawing.
  2. Type a project name in the top-left field — it stays in sync with the Project Estimator.
  3. Click Set Scale, click two known points on the drawing, then enter their real-world distance in feet to calibrate all measurements.
  4. In the Conditions panel (left), click + New to create a named measurement type (e.g. "Concrete Slab"), choose Linear, Area, or Count, and pick a color.
  5. Click a condition to select it, then draw directly on the drawing. Linear: click points, double-click to finish. Area: click corners, double-click to close. Count: single click per item.
  6. Quantities accumulate in the Quantities panel (right). Press Ctrl+Z to undo the last completed measurement, Backspace to remove the last-placed point while drawing, or Escape to cancel in-progress drawing.
  7. Use → Est on a single condition or Push All to Estimator to assign CSI divisions and unit costs, then send to the Project Estimator.
2
Project Estimator
Line-item cost estimating by CSI MasterFormat division

Build a detailed bottom-up estimate organized by construction division. Unit costs are national averages automatically adjusted for your region. Markup is applied on top to produce a final bid price.

  1. Set your project name and region in the top bar — this adjusts all unit costs.
  2. Click a CSI division in the left panel (e.g. 03 Concrete) to open it.
  3. Click + Add from Library to search and add items, or + Custom for a blank row.
  4. Enter quantities for each item — extended costs update instantly.
  5. Adjust Overhead, Profit, and Contingency % in the right panel. Also set Mat. Sales Tax % for your state and Permit Fees % (typically 0.5–2%) — both are included in the final bid price.
3
Budget Calculator
Rough order-of-magnitude estimate based on square footage

Get a fast ballpark cost range before detailed estimating begins. Useful for owner presentations, feasibility studies, or sanity-checking a bid.

  1. Select a project type (Office, Warehouse, Medical, etc.).
  2. Enter square footage, number of stories, and region.
  3. Choose a quality level — Economy, Standard, or Premium.
  4. Results appear automatically showing a cost range, $/SF, labor/material split, and a CSI division breakdown.
4
Schedule Estimator
Phased Gantt chart with milestone dates

Generate a preliminary construction schedule with phase durations and key dates. Duration is based on project type, size, and complexity.

  1. Select a project type and quality level.
  2. Enter square footage, stories, and a construction start date.
  3. A Gantt chart appears showing all 7 phases (Pre-Con through Closeout) with durations.
  4. The summary panel shows total duration, substantial completion, and final completion dates.
5
Bid Comparison
Side-by-side contractor bid analysis

Compare bids from 2–6 contractors, analyze the spread, rank by price, and compare against an engineer's estimate to evaluate whether bids are in line.

  1. Set the number of bidders (2–6) using the dropdown.
  2. Enter each contractor's name, bid amount, and any notes or qualifications.
  3. Optionally enter an Engineer's Estimate to compare against.
  4. Analysis appears automatically — spread assessment, ranking, and variance from estimate.
6
Markup & Overhead
Bid price and labor burden calculators

Two calculators in one. Project Markup builds a bid price from direct costs. Labor Burden calculates the true hourly cost of an employee including taxes and benefits.

  1. Project Markup tab: Enter direct labor, material, subcontractor, and equipment costs. Set overhead, profit, contingency, bond, and retainage rates — bid price calculates automatically. The Retainage section shows how much the owner withholds until substantial completion and your net cash received.
  2. Labor Burden tab: Enter a base wage and burden rates (FICA, FUTA, workers comp, GL, benefits) — shows fully burdened hourly rate and annual cost.
  3. Switch between tabs using the buttons at the top of the page.
7
Change Orders
Track scope and cost changes against the original contract

Change orders are written amendments to the original contract that document changes in scope, cost, or schedule. Both parties must sign before work on a change proceeds. This tool tracks every CO against your estimator's base contract value and shows the running revised total.

  1. Navigate to Change Orders in the nav — the Base Contract value pulls automatically from your Project Estimator bid price.
  2. Click + Add Change Order, enter a description, cost impact (can be negative), date, and optional scope summary.
  3. New COs start as Pending. Use the status dropdown on each row to mark them Approved or Rejected.
  4. Only approved COs add to the Revised Contract total. Pending amounts are shown separately so you can see your exposure.
8
Project Logs
RFI and submittal tracking throughout the project lifecycle

Two formal document logs in one place. RFIs (Requests for Information) document questions sent to the design team when drawings are unclear. Submittals are product data and shop drawings submitted for approval before materials are ordered. Both are written records — responses become part of the contract.

  1. RFI Log: Click + Log RFI, enter the question, who it was sent to, and the due date. Update status to Answered when the written response comes back.
  2. Submittal Log: Click + Log Submittal, enter the item description, select a CSI spec section, and the date submitted. Update to Approved, Approved as Noted, or Revise & Resubmit as the design team responds.
  3. Use the tabs at the top to switch between the RFI log and the Submittal log.
  4. Both logs share the same project data as the estimator — a Revise & Resubmit on a material may trigger a change order worth logging there too.
